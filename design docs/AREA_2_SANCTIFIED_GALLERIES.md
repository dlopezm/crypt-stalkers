# Area 2: The Sanctified Galleries

## Area overview

**Setting.** The order’s former headquarters, carved into the mine’s upper processing galleries. Largest and most varied area in the early game — where depth opens: multiple capabilities, multiple NPCs, and the dungeon’s history as lived contradiction (mine → monastery → necropolis).

**Salt economy (Area 2).** Salt replaces gold as the sole resource. Salt found here comes from order stores, ritual supplies, and personal caches. The player can spend salt to **ward rooms** (permanent enemy block + safe rest) and **fuel braziers** (hymn + salt = true light + checkpoint). Salt hoarded influences endings.

**Emotional arc.** Wonder (chapel acoustics) → learning (library, tithes, licenses) → anger (the gap between protection revenue and real protection) → unease (zombie rituals, hollowed institution) → empowerment (capabilities).

**Theme (Area 2).** **The cost of greed** — here, the **greed of authority**: a threat the order inflated, ignorance they enforced, and revenue they prioritized over the wards. The galleries show who paid: towns taxed for salt and “protection,” miners licensed and fenced out of the deep, archivists who knew the truth and could not speak.

|------------|--------|
| **FIRE** | Lamp oil (R63 Kitchen Stores) + glass flasks (R65 Workshop) → oil flasks for fire attacks and burning Skullflower |
| **RELIABLE LIGHT** | Shuttered lantern (R66 Lamp Workshop) — true flame when open, darkness when shuttered; needs lamp oil |
| **RANGED** | Crossbow + 12 bolts (R70 Weapon Racks) |

**Ritual knowledge.** Brazier relighting: hymn fragment and notation (R40 Cantor’s Stand), full hymn (R39 Choir Loft sheet music), full ritual text + brazier map (R53 Ritual Texts), Rennic’s verbal bridge (R49). Great Brazier (R41) is the major securing milestone for the Chapel Cavern.

**NPCs**

| NPC | Room | Role |
|-----|------|------|
| **Voss** (doubting cultist) | R34 Hidden Cell | Patrol intel, forbidden zones, confirms coldfire is fake light |
| **Rennic** (ghost scholar) | R49 Scholar’s Alcove | Lore, translation, hymn–brazier connection, harmonizes if player hums fragment |
| **Mira** (encounter 2) | R45 Reading Room | Trade: patrol intel + R61→R41 shortcut for non-blocking passage |

**Connections**

- **From Area 1:** Inner Gate → **R30** (Entrance Hall).
- **To Area 3 (Ossuary):** **R73** Lower Gate East — soft gate; darkness beyond favors Ghouls; **RELIABLE LIGHT** strongly recommended.
- **To Area 4 (deep workings):** **R67** Pump Room + **R68** Drain Access — requires **mine engineering docs (R48)** to operate pumps, then **FIRE** to clear Skullflower after drain. Alternate: **R74** Lower Gate West once Skullflower burned and pumps drained.
- **Backtrack gate:** **R52** Shadow Corridor — regular light consumed in 2–3 turns; pass safely only with **Crystal Lantern** (Area 4) to reach **R55** (Serevic’s pre-transformation journal).

**Light system reminder (dungeon-wide)**

- **Sunlight / true flame:** Full protection (repels Ghouls, Shadows, Larvae; suppresses Skullflower; attracts Skeleton patrols).
- **Coldfire / bioluminescence:** Fake light — visibility only; **no** protection from Ghouls, Shadows, Skullflower, Larvae.

**Thematic through-line — the Vigil Hymn.** The hymn was the one part of the order that **was not transactional**: tithes, mining licenses, recruitment, and the salt monopoly all fed the institution’s greed; the hymn served nothing and was simply beautiful. The lich ended the singing from **efficiency** — the dead cannot sing, and song does not maintain the barrier. **Banshees** carry corrupted fragments of that melody: beauty the order made, broken into a weapon. Relighting sacred flame still requires **melody** with fire, as the old rite demanded — the last echo of what greed did not price.

**What hollowed the order (readable here).** The gap between “sacred guardians” and **armed tax collectors who happened to maintain a ward**. Believers who saw the books left or died; cynics and careerists stayed. Each generation the ratio worsened; ward maintenance degraded while tithes and administration grew.

## Subarea 1: The Cloister

Dormitories and common space. Era 2 living quarters under Era 3 occupation. Zombies repeat degraded monastic routines; cultists maintain the fiction of care — another layer of service to someone else’s system.

### R30 — Entrance Hall

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombies ×2

First room past the Inner Gate. Arched Era 2 ceiling; coldfire strips retrofitted along crown. Two zombies sweep polished salt-block floor in slow circles — echo of obedience. Cracked stone; inscription: *Enter in silence, leave in service.*

- **Connects to:** Area 1 R17 (Inner Gate), R31, R37 (Chapel), R43 (Library)
- **Contains:** Order welcome plaque (lore)

### R31 — Common Room

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombies ×3, Cultist ×1

Large communal hall, stone benches. Three zombies sit with empty bowls; a cultist spoons nothing into them — kindness toward things that cannot hunger.

- **Connects to:** R30, R32, R35
- **Contains:** 12 salt (wall niche); cultist’s journal (joining, relief, horror)

### R32 — Novice Dormitory

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** Rats ×3

Long barracks: stone bed-frames, rotted bedding gone. Wall cubbies — corroded trinkets, austerity preserved in salt air. Rats in debris.

- **Connects to:** R31, R33
- **Contains:** Novice’s hidden journal (daily life, singing, hierarchy); prayer beads (minor loot)

### R33 — Knight’s Quarters

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Cultists ×2

Better rooms, some working doors. Cultist billet: surface clothes, dried food, child’s drawing. One room: makeshift lich shrine — belief made visible.

- **Connects to:** R32, R34 (hidden panel)
- **Contains:** 25 salt; dried food (minor heal); lich shrine (lore)

### R34 — Hidden Cell

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** None

Narrow cell behind loose panel in R33. Voss hides from patrol.

- **Connects to:** R33 (secret)
- **Special — Voss encounter 1:** He is not greedy in the order’s sense: three eras of extraction **emptied** the world he inherited. The town declined after the lich sealed the mine; he had no prospects. The cult offered **food**; the price is serving a monster. His “greed” is **survival** — wanting to eat — and even that binds him to the taker below. Fast talk if player is outsider: patrol timing, necromancer corridors, cultist-forbidden zones (Restricted Archive, chapel inner sanctum). Confirms coldfire is fake (*Tomasz didn’t make it*). Directions to library. Aggression → Voss flees; harder to find later.
- **Contains:** Intel as above

### R35 — Refectory

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombies ×4

Dining hall: long salt tables. Zombies circuit trays R64→tables→R64 forever. Matins bell on timer — only the living hear it. Salt-crystal cups at each place, century-empty.

- **Connects to:** R31, R36, R64 (Kitchens)
- **Contains:** Salt-crystal cups (minor loot); matins bell (deliberate ring = extreme noise lure / risk)

### R36 — Cloister Garden

**Era:** 1+2 · **Light:** Dim (ventilation shaft) · **Enemies:** None

Former herb garden; shaft admits weak daylight. Plants dead to husks; higher ceiling, hush unlike coldfire halls. Era 1 support pillar carved into Era 2 devotional column.

- **Connects to:** R35 only
- **Contains:** Dried medicinal herbs (alchemy); restful tone
- **Special:** Natural dim light — relatively safe pause point; enhanced rest healing, reduced ambush risk.

## Subarea 2: The Chapel Cavern

Natural cavern, extraordinary acoustics. Era 2 beauty vs Era 3 parody worship. Sound design is combat-relevant: noise propagates; Banshee wails amplify — the hymn’s corpse.

### R37 — Chapel Entrance

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombies ×2

Ornate arch: flame-and-voice motif. Zombies flank like ushers. Beyond, cavern opens — every footstep and clash gains resonance.

- **Connects to:** R30, R38
- **Special:** Chapel acoustic rules — combat noise spreads to adjacent chapel rooms; Banshee screams amplified; can alert Ghosts in Library block (R43–R50)

### R38 — Nave

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Cultists ×2, Zombies ×3

Vast nave: salt-crystal walls vanishing upward, pews carved from floor. Cultists chant; zombies sit — drone almost beautiful in the stone. Stalactites carved as singing saints, mouths open forever — relic of when song was not on the ledger.

- **Connects to:** R37, R39, R40, R41, R42
- **Contains:** Offering bowls (18 salt total); damaged hymnals — one legible notation (hymn fragment clue)

### R39 — Choir Loft

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** Banshee ×1

Elevated platform; salt stairs from R38. Figure in choir robes, mouth frozen open. Wail corrupts hymn recognizable if player saw R38 hymnal. Music stands, some with sheet music.

- **Connects to:** R38 (stairs)
- **Contains:** **Complete hymn sheet** (brazier ritual); 15 salt (conductor’s box)
- **Special:** Wail echoes R37–R42; triggers Ghosts in Library; plan ranged silence or accept cascade

### R40 — Cantor’s Stand

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** None

Small raised platform for lead singer. Inscription: brazier-lighting ceremony — *At the crescendo of the Vigil Hymn, the keeper strikes the brazier’s heart and sings the note of waking* — plus notation. Rosetta for singing + fire + faith — the rite the institution later judged useless.

- **Connects to:** R38
- **Contains:** **Hymn fragment** (core relighting key — combine with R39, R53, Rennic)
- **Special:** Ties brazier system to order practice explicitly

### R41 — Great Brazier Platform

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Dark (brazier out) · **Enemies:** Witch ×1

Central dais. **Great Brazier:** cart-scale salt-crystal basin, flame-and-voice relief. Witch performs over cold hearth; coldfire orbits her, not the bowl.

- **Connects to:** R38, R61 (Upper Passage — drop/entry from above)
- **Contains:** Great Brazier (environmental objective)
- **Special — Great Brazier milestone:** Defeat Witch first. Relight with full procedure (hymn + R40/R53 knowledge + performed melody) → **entire Chapel Cavern** gains true light: Skullflower suppressed here; Ghouls/Larvae flee; Shadows cannot enter; Skeleton patrols from Area 1 add chapel route. Largest transform in Areas 1–2.

### R42 — Side Chapel

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** Ghost ×1 (ex-priest)

Quiet alcove; altar to patron saint of miners. Ghost kneels, murmuring unheard. Salt offerings; small painting — mine mouth in gold light (order’s self-image as protectors, not collectors).

- **Connects to:** R38
- **Contains:** Salt (crafting); 8 salt (offering box); painting (lore)
- **Special:** Quiet approach → loot/observe without fight. Banshee wail (R39) can activate this Ghost unexpectedly.

## Subarea 3: The Library

Order’s public and indexed knowledge. Ghost-haunted; **quiet play** avoids Ghost combat. Primary lore density before Restricted Archive — and the trail of **documents** that prove exploitation was specific and filed.

### R43 — Library Entrance

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombies ×2

Arch: *SEEK AND BE ILLUMINATED.* Zombies mime shelving vanished books. Mostly empty wooden racks.

- **Connects to:** R30, R44, R47

### R44 — Public Stacks

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Ghosts ×2

Long galleries; some volumes survived in salt air. Ghosts reshelve endlessly — harmless unless noise.

- **Connects to:** R43, R45, R46
- **Contains:** Training theology/history (biased toward “darkness” warnings, not geology); meditation manual (minor combat buff if studied)
- **Special:** **Quiet zone** — adjacent fights can wake them

### R45 — Reading Room

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Ghost ×1

Desks; dead reading lamps. Mira crouched behind furniture.

- **Connects to:** R44
- **Special — Mira encounter 2:** Seeks text on restricted vault / deeper treasure. Trade: patrol windows Areas 1–2, **shortcut R61→R41**, for player not blocking her exits through Upper Galleries. Hostile → she escapes; lose trade.
- **Contains:** Partial map / patrol markup if allied; 10 salt she meant to steal

### R46 — Order Archive

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Ghost ×1 (archivist)

Administrative core: ledgers, deeds, the **paper spine** of authority’s greed. **Salt tithe records** — annual tribute from each town for “blessed salt” (ordinary salt with a prayer) and for protection from wards the payers never saw. **Mining license fee schedules** — who paid what to work which upper galleries; deep galleries listed as forbidden while the order reserved the purest product for itself. **Revenue reports** — decades of rising income beside **declining ward-maintenance spending**; the gap between “protection” money and what was actually spent on protection is visible in the columns. **Ashvere acquisition file** — mine bought for twelve silver marks, heirs stripped, margin notes: *grasping*, *deluded by entitlement.*

- **Connects to:** R44
- **Contains:** **Ashvere file** (anger beat; lich dialogue hook on claim); documents as above; 15 salt (strongbox)

### R47 — Catalog Room

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** None

Indexes. Entry: Restricted Archive — titles include *On the Persistence of the Self Beyond Mortality*, *Confessions of the Chapter*; also *Relighting of Sacred Flame — Full Ritual* (restricted). Cross-reference cards hint at **classified** surveys and ward schedules the public never saw.

- **Connects to:** R43, R48, R49, R50

### R48 — Mining Records

**Era:** 1+2 · **Light:** Dim · **Enemies:** None

Baron-era docs preserved: maps, geology, pumps, **crystal reflector arrays** (Area 4). **Geological surveys** chart the deep presence indirectly — barrier thickness by stratum, resonance notes. Early surveys show **thick** natural containment; later surveys show **thinning** in the same sections. **Order records** show **resumed mining of upper salt** for income after the early ward-building phase — extraction timelines that **correlate** with the thinning columns (same years, same galleries). Baron’s investor letters — public optimism vs private dread (cross-ref journals elsewhere).

- **Connects to:** R47
- **Contains:** **Mine engineering documents** (R67 pump puzzle); **reflector documentation** (Area 4); surveys and correlation notes (lore); investor reports (lore)

### R49 — Scholar’s Alcove

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** None (Rennic — NPC ghost, non-hostile)

Study niche. Rennic: faint glow, books open. He **catalogued** the order’s records for a lifetime, saw the gap between the public story (“darkness rises”) and the restricted truth (surveys, schedules, real numbers). He could not speak up in any way that mattered — the order **owned the narrative**. He paid the **cost of the order’s greed** without ever profiting from it.

- **Connects to:** R47
- **Special — Prior Rennic:** Explains found texts; translates inscriptions; library wayfinding; **hymn–brazier link** (*The flame answered to the voice…*); asks after surface and song. Player hums R40 fragment → Rennic harmonizes; brief warm light — *Pale Vigil.* Key line: *“Your family built this. And this place destroyed them for it.”*
- **Contains:** Rennic’s notes (transformation context — Ending 2 relevant); emotional anchor

### R50 — Restricted Section Door

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** None

Grandmaster seal; locked. Key: **Grandmaster’s Library Seal** (R59). Inscription: *Beyond lies knowledge too heavy for the uninitiated.* Keyhole: dark, something moves — Shadow in R52. Behind the door: the hoarded truth — threat’s nature, geology, maintenance — everything the tithes were sold on but never shown.

- **Connects to:** R47 → **R51** when unlocked

## Subarea 4: The Restricted Archive

Highest-value texts. Coldfire antechamber; **Shadow corridor** gates the deepest prize until crystal lantern backtrack. **Knowledge hoarding** made literal: geology, wards, and ritual in one locked wing.

### R51 — Restricted Vestibule

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Cultist ×1

Archivist checkpoint desk. Guard can be fought, sneaked (cultist disguise from Voss route), or bluffed with library seal (R59) as authorized entry.

- **Connects to:** R50, R52, R53, R54

### R52 — Shadow Corridor

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** Shadow ×1

Long hall to R55. Shadow **eats** normal carried light — torch/lantern (non-crystal) dies in **2–3 turns**. Far end: cabinet room R55.

- **Connects to:** R51, R55
- **Special:** **Crystal Lantern backtrack gate** — first visit forces retreat after light dies; return from Area 4 with crystal lantern → Shadow cannot consume it; fight or bypass to R55.

### R53 — Ritual Texts

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** None

Ceremonial vault. **Full brazier relighting** (melody, gesture, invocation). **Consecration rite** — knowledge half of **CONSECRATION** (relic: Area 3 R102). **Architectural brazier map** — all sacred braziers dungeon-wide.

- **Connects to:** R51
- **Contains:** Items as above

### R54 — Order Confessions

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** None

Internal record of exploitation made routine: **tithe skimming** ledgers (what reached the vault vs what the towns were told), **land seizure** files (plots “donated” under spiritual pressure), **recruitment quotas** by village (*Send your children, or the darkness rises* — best to wards, most to administration). **Internal audits** track **administrative staff growth** against **warrior-class decline** — the institution feeding itself while the hands on the wards thinned. Standout: dissident knight — *The vigil has become its own purpose — we guard nothing and profit from the guarding.* Annotation: *Expelled. Let the salt take his doubts.*

- **Connects to:** R51
- **Contains:** Confession corpus; documents as above; dissident letter (foreshadows lich themes)

### R55 — Serevic’s Pre-Transformation Journal

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark (via Shadow zone) · **Enemies:** None

Locked cabinet past R52. **Not** a grief journal about the order “dying” — it is **Serevic’s working mind** while still human: **barrier calculations**, measurements, the math that proved thinning. **Council meeting minutes** where they presented degradation data and were told to **raise the tithe**. **Degradation reports** marked received but **unread** in the margins. **Cost-benefit analysis** leading to the transformation — dead workforce, no food, no rest, immortal oversight — laid out in the same careful hand as the surveys. The **mathematical proof** that the barrier would fail in **30–50 years** at then-current rates. One **undated** entry breaks the pattern: *The evening hymn was particularly fine today. I wonder if that matters.*

**Ending 4 (Release):** Presenting this journal to Serevic in Area 5 forces them to confront **their own doubt** — the line their calculus could not file.

- **Connects to:** R52
- **Contains:** **Serevic’s pre-transformation journal** — transforms lich dialogue; optional placement in Area 5 mortal quarters for extra beat
- **Special:** Requires **Crystal Lantern** path through R52; premier backtrack reward

### R57 — Grandmaster’s Study

- **Connects to:** R56

### R58 — Scribe’s Room

**Era:** 2+3 · **Light:** Coldfire · **Enemies:** Zombie ×1

- **Connects to:** R56

### R59 — Seal Room

### R60 — Portrait Gallery

### R61 — Upper Passage

**Era:** 2 · **Light:** Dark · **Enemies:** None
