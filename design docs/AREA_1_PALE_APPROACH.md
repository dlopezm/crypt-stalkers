# Area 1: The Pale Approach

## Context

**Setting:** The Pale Vault — a salt mine turned monastery turned necropolis. The player is the last heir of the baron who built the mine, entering to reclaim their inheritance.

**Theme (Area 1):** The cost of greed. Greed has a price; it is rarely paid by the one who profits. This wing introduces the family name on ledgers and contracts — and the first evidence that the inheritance was bought with other people's futures.

**Era visual identity**

- **Era 1 (mine, ~400 yrs):** Raw stone, timber bracing, iron cart tracks, salt crystal. Functional, honest; workmanship gets slapdash at depth.
- **Era 2 (order, ~250 yrs):** Clean salt-block walls, arched corridors, carved inscriptions, flame motifs. Austere but beautiful.
- **Era 3 (lich, ~150 yrs):** Bone, coldfire strips, industrial layout. Organized and uncanny.

**Light system (four types)**

| Type | Examples | Visibility | Protection |
|------|-----------|------------|------------|
| **Sunlight** | Ventilation shafts, open sky | Full | Full |
| **True flame** | Torches, lanterns, braziers | Full | Full |
| **Coldfire** | Lich’s green necromantic glow | Yes | **No** — fake light |
| **Bioluminescence** | Skullflower glow | Yes | **No** — fake light |

**Protection** = repels Ghouls and Shadows, suppresses Skullflower growth, repels Gutborn Larvae. Coldfire and bioluminescence do **not** grant protection even when the player can see clearly.

## Area overview

**Scope:** The mine entrance and the order’s ruined gatehouse, blending into shallow underground. First taste of all three eras in one area.

**Emotional arc:** Recognition (crest, foreman’s log, family door) → discomfort (indentured contracts, ignored safety, journals — the legacy is not clean) → triumph (return with blunt damage; the heir can survive what the mine became).

**Learning sequence**

- Skeletons **reform one turn after death** unless finished with **blunt** damage. They are **workers who cannot stop working** — the lich’s perpetual shift; blunt damage is the only way to end labor that was never meant to end.
- **True light** (sun / flame) attracts skeleton patrols that **see in the dark** and **investigate light**; planning routes and timing matters.
- **Dark** side spaces hide **salt, lore, and shortcuts** — risk vs. reward without reliable protection from fake light.
- **Coldfire** reads as “the mine is lit” but teaches the hard lesson: green glow is **not** safety (reinforced by Mira’s line).
- **Zombies** here **follow fixed routes and orders** — **workers reduced to instructions**; the system runs while someone else’s greed insists it must.
- **Lore seed:** The **indentured labor contracts** in the foreman’s office — first sign that the family legacy is not clean.

**What Lies Below (seed):** The deep salt sits on a geological weak point — vast, patient pressure. The purest, most valuable salt lies closest to that thin place; **the treasure and the danger are the same substance.** Mining thinned containment. Miners spoke of heat, sounds, bad dreams in the lowest galleries; the baron wrote that superstition does not appear on the ledger. The catastrophic collapse came when a deep gallery broke a **critical salt stratum** — the presence **surged**; hundreds died; rubble **re-sealed** the breach. Area 1 hints at this through warmth and resonance in the deep-facing salt, not through lectures.

**NPC beats**

- **Mira (Grave Robber), encounter 1 — Junction Hall (R15):** Fleeing through the crossroads; freezes on seeing the player, bolts toward R14. Drops a **healing potion**. Shouts: *“The green light doesn’t keep them away!”* Gone before dialogue. If R14 is already clear, pursuit is possible in fiction — she’s faster and vanishes into the Excursion Warrens.

**Starting equipment:** The player enters with only a knife. Everything else — weapons, consumables, abilities — is found in the dungeon. The knife is adequate against rats but not much else; the first real weapon upgrade is the **rusty shortsword** at R8 (wrong damage type for skeletons — teaches damage types).

**Salt economy (Area 1):** Salt replaces gold as the sole resource. Salt deposits, order stores, and miner caches provide salt. The player can spend salt to **ward rooms** (permanent enemy block, safe rest) or **fuel braziers** (true light, checkpoint). Hoarded salt influences endings — more salt kept = greed. R5 has a functioning **order ward** the player can observe before learning to create their own (Area 2+).

**Key items & progression**

| Item / beat | Location | Role |
|-------------|----------|------|
| **Mining maul** | R26 Tool Cache | Primary **BLUNT** capability; baron’s tool; best blunt in game; later mining / breakable walls. |
| **Rusty mace** | R16 Patrol Station | Alternate **BLUNT** — weaker, no Baron’s Wing required. |
| **Baron’s Cache** | R28 Family Vault | **Pick-hammer** (unique blunt + piercing), **80 salt**, **signet ring** (Ashvere proof, dialogue later), **journal / letter** (emotional capstone). |
| **Tuning fork** | R27 Baron’s Study | Quiet option for **Inner Gate (R17)** resonance lock. |
| **Inner Gate (R17)** | — | **Soft gate to Area 2** (R30): lever + handplate + resonance (fork, hinted hum, or loud bash). |

**Family throughline**

- **Ashvere crest** on entrance arch (R1); order defaced it but it remains recognizable.
- **Surname** in **foreman’s log** (R2) as shift supervisor — tied to an operation that signed others into debt.
- **Baron’s Wing** (R24–R29): motto gate, portraits, journals, vault, **baron’s** letter (*If the mine takes me, don’t come back for it. Let it go.*) — distinct from the **ancestor-miner’s** warning in Area 4 (*Don’t come looking for what we left here. The salt keeps its own.*).

**Baron’s arithmetic (lore spine):** He took **debts to expand**; surface veins **thinned**; **deeper salt** was richer — purer, stranger — but costlier and riskier; **other salt sources undercut prices**; **creditors demanded payment**, so **extraction had to deepen**. Not melodramatic villainy — **arithmetic.**

## Subarea 1: The Mine Mouth

Surface entrance; daylight fades with depth. Era 1 dominates; Era 2 plaster overlays; Era 3 patrol markers and first coldfire tease deeper threat.

### R1 — Entrance Arch

**START · Era 1 + Era 2 plaster · Sunlit · No enemies**

The mine mouth opens under a defaced Ashvere crest — chisel marks from the order, but the pickaxe, crystal, and mountain still read as family. Iron cart tracks bite into the floor and run inward. Above the arch, the baron’s motto survives in fragments: *From the earth, prosperity.* You stand where a fortune was dug from stone — and where the bill was charged to people who did not keep the books.

- **Connects to:** R2 (Weighing Station), R7 (Outer Court), surface (exit).
- **Contains:** Readable crest and motto (lore + later gate hints); visual read on era layering.
- **Special:** Start room; safe introduction; no combat.

---

### R2 — Weighing Station

**Era 1 · Dim (some daylight) · Rats ×2**

A low stone platform where carts were weighed; rusted scales and chains; a shelf holds a water-stained **foreman’s log**. Shift rosters name the player’s **surname** as supervisor — proof this place was **theirs**, and that their name sat above crews whose terms you have not yet read.

- **Connects to:** R1, R3.
- **Contains:** **Foreman’s log** (family tie); **5 salt** (miner’s pay, crystallized).
- **Special:** Teaches that documents reward thorough search; rats introduce breeding / door-squeeze later.

---

### R3 — Cart Depot

**Era 1 · Dim · Rats ×3**

A wide chamber for storage and cart repair. Several carts remain; one is heaped with **raw salt** as if recently moved — implication: Era 3 logistics still touch the mouth. Tracks continue toward the galleries and gatehouse branches. The **deepest-looking** salt chunks catch the dim light oddly; sometimes the wall-facing cartloads feel a trace **warm**, as if the stone remembered a pulse from below.

- **Connects to:** R2, R4, R5, R6.
- **Contains:** **Salt blocks** (crafting / quest later); **8 salt**.
- **Special:** Hub room; player chooses foreman’s office, safe shaft, or darker threshold first.

---

### R4 — Foreman’s Office

**Era 1 + Era 2 vandalism · Dim · No enemies**

A pocket office carved into the wall. The **baron’s portrait** hangs crooked: eyes scratched, *GREED* carved across the chest — order judgment frozen in paint and knife. The desk and filing niche are thick with **indentured labor contracts**: workers charged for **housing, tools, food**; interest and fees structured so the **principal never shrank**. Signatures repeat; the same surnames appear for years. Pinned beneath them, **safety petitions** from the **miners’ guild** — ventilation, bracing, pace in the deep — each stamped **REVIEWED** and **filed**, with no amendment, no repair date, no answer.

- **Connects to:** R3 only.
- **Contains:** **Mine maps** (hint deep layout / connections); **indentured contracts**; **guild safety petitions** (REVIEWED, no action); **12 salt** in a **locked drawer** (key elsewhere or lockpick).
- **Special:** Lore density; first proof the heir’s prosperity had a line item paid in someone else’s life.

---

### R5 — Ventilation Shaft Base

**Era 1 + Era 2 · Lit (sunlight) · No enemies**

A vertical shaft threads pale daylight and fresh air from above; too narrow to climb. At the base stands an **Era 2 brazier** — extinguished, one of the first sacred fire fixtures the player sees.

- **Connects to:** R3.
- **Contains:** **Brazier** (relightable after **Area 2** ritual).
- **Special:** **Safe room** — sunlight blocks most monster entry; enhanced rest healing; no ambush risk. The **order ward** inscribed around the brazier base is the player’s first sight of ward markings — they will later learn to create these themselves.

---

### R6 — Gallery Threshold

**Era 1 + first Era 3 coldfire · Dim → dark · Skeletons ×1 (patrol with R12)**

Ceiling drops; cart tracks continue. The air is not quite as cold as the depth suggests — a **faint warmth** threads the draft, wrong for a shallow mine. **Coldfire strips** mar the walls — first sight of the lich’s light. In places the salt in the seam **hums** almost below hearing when the skeleton’s boots strike stone. One skeleton walks a loop into **R12** — **perpetual shift** — and this is the first undead fight and the first place players may see a **reform** if they lack blunt.

- **Connects to:** R3, R12.
- **Contains:** Environmental teach: fake light visible ahead in Main Gallery.
- **Special:** **First skeleton**; **first coldfire**; subtle **warmth / resonance** — unease, not exposition; bridges Mine Mouth to Upper Galleries.

## Subarea 2: The Gatehouse

Fortified order entrance over the mine mouth. Era 2 architecture; Era 3 neglect and surface-facing undead activity.

### R7 — Outer Court

**Era 2 + ruin · Lit (open sky) · No enemies**

Courtyard before the inner gatehouse; outer wall crumbles. Gate pillars wear carvings: **flame** and **open mouths** (singing / vigil). A **dead Grave Robber** lies near the threshold — broken by a skeleton patrol, pack spilled.

- **Connects to:** R1, R8, R9.
- **Contains:** **Dead Grave Robber’s pack** — **torch ×2**, **15 salt**, **small healing salve** (first consumable), **crude map of Area 1** (annotated danger / loot).
- **Special:** Early gear + first consumable; foreshadows Grave Robber behavior and patrol danger.

---

### R8 — Guard Room

**Era 2 + neglect · Dim · Rats ×2**

Gate guards once stood here. Empty weapon rack (long looted). A **duty roster** pinned with rusted nails lists shifts from ~200 years ago — readable patterns echo skeleton patrol logic.

- **Connects to:** R7.
- **Contains:** **Guard duty roster** (patrol timing hints); **rusted shortsword** (weak slashing — **not** blunt).
- **Special:** Wrong weapon type teaches damage-type lesson if player experiments on skeletons.

---

### R9 — Receiving Hall

**Era 2 · Dim · Zombies ×2 (non-hostile route)**

Once-grand reception space. Faded frescoes: figures over a mine mouth, raised hands, **flames above palms** — founding myth of the order. Inscription: *We who keep the vigil stand between the salt and the shadow.* Two **zombies** lug salt blocks on a fixed path: **R9 → R7 → surface** (and return), **reduced to instructions** — minding their task unless attacked.

- **Connects to:** R7, R10, R11.
- **Contains:** **Frescoes** (ideology, **singing** motif — later resonance hint for R17).
- **Special:** **Organized undead** — shows command structure; player can observe route without fight.

---

### R10 — Record Room

**Era 2 · Dark · No enemies**

Small archive off R9. Most folios pulp; one sheet survives: a **receipt** — the order bought *the Ashvere mining concession* for *twelve silver marks and the promise of spiritual stewardship.* Nearby letters sneer at *the Ashvere claim.*

- **Connects to:** R9.
- **Contains:** **Acquisition receipt** (family cheated); **order correspondence**; **6 salt**.
- **Special:** Dark room — true light or risk; emotional beat: the heir was sold cheap.

---

### R11 — Gatehouse Balcony

**Era 2 · Lit (open air) · Bats ×3**

Stairs from R9. Elevated walk; player looks down into **R1** and **R7** without being seen from those rooms if careful. **Skeleton patrol** visible below on its loop — timing and composition readable from safety.

- **Connects to:** R9 (stairs only).
- **Contains:** Vantage over Mine Mouth and court.
- **Special:** **Observation tutorial** — learn patrol path before engaging; bats teach door-blocking / vertical nuisance.

## Subarea 3: The Upper Galleries

Main mine corridors; skeleton patrols; Era 1 tunnels under cracking Era 2 plaster; Era 3 coldfire overhead. Deeper-facing walls occasionally carry that same **wrong warmth** and **almost-tone** in the salt when the mine is quiet.

### R12 — Main Gallery

**Era 1 + Era 2 plaster + Era 3 coldfire · Coldfire · Skeletons ×2 (patrol)**

Wide corridor; iron tracks; plaster flakes to raw stone. **Coldfire** runs the ceiling — bright, wrong, **no protection** vs. threats that care about true light (Area 1 roster is light on those, but player learns the rule for later). Patrol route: **R12 → R15 → R17 → R15 → R12** (skeletons on **perpetual** loop).

- **Connects to:** R6, R13, R14, R15.
- **Contains:** Patrol pressure; coldfire as default “dungeon lighting.”
- **Special:** Core traffic; noise / light here pulls investigating skeletons from adjacent logic.

---

### R13 — Side Gallery East

**Era 1 · Dark · Rats ×4**

Narrow east branch; rat nests in rubble. **Miner graffiti:** tallies, names, rat sketch with X. A folk **salt saint** shrine — carved figure, **headless**.

- **Connects to:** R12.
- **Contains:** **10 salt** in miner’s tin behind shrine; **salt figure** (flavor item).
- **Special:** Dark reward lane; clearing nests later ties to rat breeding rules.

---

### R14 — Side Gallery West

**Era 1 + recent breach · Dark · Rats ×2**

Narrows toward Excursion Warrens. **Fresh dirt**, pick marks — Era 3 forced entry. Order plaque: *SEALED BY DECREE* — ignored.

- **Connects to:** R12, R19.
- **Special:** **Mira escape vector**; risky exploration without flame; links to surface supply story (warrens).

---

### R15 — Junction Hall

**Era 2 signage + defacement · Coldfire · Skeletons ×1 (patrol), Grave Robber ×1 (Mira)**

High-ceiling crossroads; broken directional sign: *Chapel / Library / Quarters* — all deeper. **Mira** crosses with a sack; on player entry she **freezes**, then **sprints for R14**, drops **healing potion**, shouts *The green light doesn’t keep them away!* and is gone.

- **Connects to:** R12, R16, R17, R24.
- **Contains:** **Healing potion** (dropped).
- **Special:** **Mira encounter 1**; coldfire irony; hub fights and family door (R24).

---

### R16 — Patrol Station

**Era 3 + mine shell · Coldfire · Skeletons ×2 (idle / rest between loops)**

Side chamber where patrol skeletons bunch between circuits — **between shifts that never end**. Weapon rack holds **rusty mace** — usable **blunt**. Wall carving: **patrol schedule** (explicit timing for R12–R15–R17 loop).

- **Connects to:** R15.
- **Contains:** **Rusty mace** (early blunt); **patrol schedule**.
- **Special:** **Alternate BLUNT** if Baron’s Wing skipped; stealth entry vs. two skeletons.

---

### R17 — The Inner Gate

**Era 2 salt-block door + Era 3 occupation · Coldfire · Skeletons ×2 (guards)**

Massive door: flame carvings, salt blocks, working mechanism. **Three parts:** obvious **lever**, **carved handplate**, **resonance lock** (pitch / fork / voice). Guards do not operate it — they only kill intruders.

- **Connects to:** R15, R18; **→ Area 2, R30** when open.
- **Contains:** Gate interaction; musical **notation scratch** near lock (links R9 frescoes).
- **Special:** **Progression gate (soft).** Resolve by: **(a)** tuning fork from **R27**, **(b)** correct hum (hints from frescoes + scratches), **(c)** **bash** — loud, damages lock, **alerts Upper Gallery skeletons**. Skilled observation = quiet pass; brute force = costly pass.

---

### R18 — Brazier Alcove

**Era 2 · Dark (niche) · No enemies**

Small recess beside R17. **Ornate sacred brazier**, out; **salt-crystal inlays** catch any light the player carries — last true flame before the order’s deep domain.

- **Connects to:** R17.
- **Contains:** **Sacred brazier** (relight Area 2 ritual).
- **Special:** When **lit**, creates **true-light safe zone** spanning **R17–R18**; skeleton patrols **redirect** to **investigate**, changing gallery traffic — tactical map shift.

## Subarea 4: The Excursion Warrens

Fresh Era 3 digs: raw earth, timber, no salt-block finesse. Tunnels the order never authorized; lich’s route toward surface supply.

### R19 — Warren Entrance

**Era 3 excavation · Dark · Zombies ×2 (hauling)**

Rough mouth off R14; **new timber**, **fresh picks**. **Zombies** haul **salt sacks inward** from the surface chain — **instructions in motion** — same non-hostile rule unless struck.

- **Connects to:** R14, R20, R21; long run to **R23**.
- **Special:** **Supply chain reveal** — undead economy visible; another face of **cost** kept off someone else’s ledger.

---

### R20 — Dig Face

**Era 3 · Dark · No enemies**

Active end of the cut. Picks stuck in mud; tunnel **stops mid-bore**. **Skeletal footprints** in wet earth — crew walked away or still works elsewhere.

- **Connects to:** R19.
- **Contains:** **Pickaxes** (tools, poor weapons); environmental storytelling.
- **Special:** Implies ongoing expansion elsewhere in the vault.

---

### R21 — Supply Cache

**Era 3 widened chamber · Dark · Rats ×3, Grave Robber ×1**

Stolen goods pile: **salt**, **household plunder**, **church candles**. A **Grave Robber** loots by **torch** (true light). Flees toward **R22** if spotted.

- **Connects to:** R19, R22.
- **Contains:** **Salt sacks**; **20 salt**; **candles** (portable light); **village salt-cellar receipt** (raids for salt).
- **Special:** Robber **surrenders** if cornered in R22: *There’s a sealed wing off the junction — old family stuff. The skeletons don’t go in there.* (Points to **R24**.)

### R22 — Collapsed Tunnel

**Era 3 collapse · Dark · Rats ×5**

Side passage **caved**; shattered timbers; **rat colony**. Collapse may tie to warren blasting.

- **Connects to:** R21 only (dead end).
- **Contains:** **Rat nests** — clearing reduces / stops breeding **in this room**.
- **Special:** **Dead end**; flee target for R21 robber; nest-clearing gameplay hook.

### R23 — Surface Breach

**Era 3 · Lit (daylight) · No enemies**

Hillside **rip**; daylight and wind; distant fields visible. **Skeletal tracks** lead out — undead use this daily. Player **can exit** to wilderness — **not** toward town; narrative beat: the plague road is real.

- **Connects to:** R19 (long tunnel).
- **Special:** **Alternate exit**; **safe room** (sun); reinforces stakes of surface raids.

## Subarea 5: The Baron’s Wing

Sealed family enclave. Era 1 workmanship at its best; order sealed it; lich ignored. ~400 years of dust; no coldfire inside — **dark** until the player brings flame.

### R24 — Sealed Door

**Junction side: coldfire bleed from R15 · No enemies (until opened)**

Iron door: **Ashvere crest** — pickaxe crossed with **salt crystal** over **mountain**. Lock expects **family motto** (player knowledge or read through crack: *From the earth, prosperity* — matches **R1**).

- **Connects to:** R15 (out), R25 (in).
- **Special:** **Family / identity gate**, not a skill gate. Opening it hushes the wing — pristine, untouched, **dark**.

---

### R25 — Baron’s Gallery

**Era 1 premium · Dark · No enemies**

Private corridor; **polished salt-crystal** walls (dust-choked); finest stonework in Area 1. **Salt-panel portraits:** young hopeful baron; older harder baron; family group smiling. Inscription: *The Ashvere Line — Builders of the White Galleries.* The crystal here **rings** faintly when struck; in stillness it almost seems to **answer** something far down and out of sight.

- **Connects to:** R24, R26, R27, R28, R29.
- **Contains:** **Portraits** — face of the ancestor; emotional mirror for the heir.
- **Special:** Hub for tool, study, vault, and chasm.

---

### R26 — Tool Cache

**Era 1 · Dark · No enemies**

Personal mining store. Most iron rusted through. Exception: **mining maul** — heavy head, **salt-crystal inlay**, **Ashvere crest** on haft; crystal arrested rust.

- **Connects to:** R25.
- **Contains:** **MINING MAUL** — **primary BLUNT capability**; best blunt weapon; future **mining / breakable wall** interactions.
- **Special:** Intended **“come back prepared”** weapon; validates return after first skeleton reform lesson; **triumph** beat after discomfort in R4 / R27.

---

### R27 — Baron’s Study

**Era 1 · Dark · No enemies**

Desk, chair, shelves of decayed ledgers. **Three journal volumes** trace **debt spiral**, not vague ambition:

- **Volume 1 — Idealism:** Discovery of the deposit; **schools for miners’ children**; **fair wages**; pride in honest work.
- **Volume 2 — Arithmetic:** **Market competition**; **debts to capital creditors**; **cutting costs**; the conviction that ***the deeper veins will make us whole***.
- **Volume 3 — The ledger:** **Indentured labor** rationalized; **safety warnings** and miners’ fears dismissed — ***Superstition does not appear on the ledger.*** Eve-of-collapse entry: **tomorrow the lower gallery breaks through**; confidence mixed with hurry.

**Tuning fork** in a drawer — baron’s pitch.

- **Connects to:** R25.
- **Contains:** **Journals** (greed-as-arithmetic arc); **tuning fork** (**R17** resonance solution).
- **Special:** Lore bridges to deep areas; fork rewards thorough search.

---

### R28 — Family Vault

**Era 1 reinforced · Dark · No enemies**

Inner door: **combination** — motto again or sequence from **journals**. Inside: concentrated reward and punch.

- **Connects to:** R25.
- **Contains (BARON’S CACHE — bonus):** **Pick-hammer** (unique **blunt + piercing**, mythic tool-of-the-mine); **80 salt**; **baron’s signet ring** (Ashvere proof, future dialogue); **letter to child (the baron):** *If the mine takes me, don’t come back for it. Let it go.* — the player violates the warning. (Not the Area 4 ancestor-miner letter.)
- **Special:** **Emotional climax** of Area 1; baron knew the cost and went anyway; heir repeats the pattern.

---

### R29 — Collapse Point

**Era 1 edge / void · Dark · No enemies**

Wing ends at a **chasm** — the **400-year collapse** that killed the baron and hundreds. It was not **only** timber and bad luck: the deep gallery had **broken a critical salt stratum**; **strange heat** welled from below; the **presence surged**; then **rubble re-sealed** the breach. Far down: shattered **lower gallery** hints. The updraft is **warm** — wrong for a mine shaft. Sometimes a **faint resonance** rises from the dark, as if the salt were still **transmitting** what happened that day. Standing here, the void reads less as empty rock than as **something still down there**, held back by thickness and time — for now.

- **Connects to:** R25.
- **Special:** **Foreshadow What Lies Below**; optional interaction: drop lit torch — long fall, small impact flash — sells **real depth**; not a passage in Area 1; later **rope / engineering (Area 4)** may link to **Deep Workings**.

## Implementation notes (quick reference)

- **Skeleton reform:** 1-turn delay unless killing blow is **blunt**; teach in R6/R12 before Mira’s coldfire warning fully lands.
- **Light attraction:** True light sources alert / draw skeleton **investigation**; coldfire does not protect — only clarifies Mira’s line when paired with bestiary from later areas.
- **Zombie routes:** Script waypoints R9↔R7 and R19 inward; attack toggles hostility.
- **Inner gate:** Implement three failure modes for resonance + bash debuff + alert radius.
- **Braziers R5/R18:** Gate relight behind Area 2 ritual flag; R18 lit state toggles patrol graph.
- **R29:** One-way look / item drop; no descent until flag + item from later area.
