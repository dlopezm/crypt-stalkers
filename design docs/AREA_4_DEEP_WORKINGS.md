# Area 4: The Deep Workings

**Game:** The Pale Vault — salt mine, monastery, necropolis. The player is the last heir of the baron who built the mine.

**Theme (this area):** **The Cost of Greed** — and its counterweight. Here the player learns the mine's **original sin** and its **natural solution**. The treasure and the danger are the **same substance**: the deep presence refines the salt around it; the purest, warmest, most luminous salt sits closest to the threat. Mining that salt thins the containment. **The regrowing salt** — in galleries the baron's crews never gutted, or abandoned long ago, crystal **heals**: gaps fill, seams thicken, geology repairs itself. If the taking stops, containment is enough. That discovery is the key evidence for **Ending 4 (Release)** — the fact the lich could not afford to measure.

---

## Light System (Reference)

| Source | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Sunlight / crystal arrays | Full protection; kills Shadows |
| True flame | Full protection; repels Shadows |
| Coldfire | Fake; no protection |
| Bioluminescence (Skullflower) | Fake; no protection |
| **Crystal lantern** (crafted in Area 4) | Counts as sunlight; immune to Shadow drain |

**Crystal lantern (thematic):** Crafted from the mine's **own** material — salt that was here before anyone came to take. The oldest substance in the place, shaped and **channeled through the player's will**, becomes the strongest light in the game. Not imported sanctity: the earth's own crystal, saving the explorer from the void its depths invited. The mine offers rescue through **what it already was**.

---

## Area Overview

The mine's oldest, deepest tunnels. Sealed by the order ~250 years ago. Partially reopened by the lich, then abandoned when crews vanished. Wilderness under the institution: dark, unstable, colonized by Shadows and Skullflower — and, in the untouched places, **living salt**.

**Emotional arc:** Awe (crystal caverns) → **revelation** (regrowth; profit = peril) → solitude → weight (ancestor who understood "stop") → mastery (reflector arrays) → dread and **clarity** (seal, observation log, demon).

**Core mechanic:** Crystal reflector arrays — multi-room spatial puzzles (clean mirrors, clear obstructions, route light, watch sections transform). Shadow light-drain forces timing and the crystal lantern as the durable solution.

**Capability earned:** **Crystal lantern** — crafted at **R138** from salt crystal + existing lantern + knowledge (notes / Area 2 mining records / reflector work).

**Key beats:** **Regrowing salt** (Crystal Galleries — escalates R117 → R121/R123 with a **specific proof moment**). **Mira encounter 3** (R127) — mirror of the heir; honest greed. **Crystal Master Array** bonus (R122 — permanent light across Crystal Galleries and into Shadow Depths). **Ancestor's Alcove** (R134) — letter that reframes the whole theme. **Demon Seal** decision (R143).

**Salt economy (Area 4).** The mine's product IS the resource, and here it becomes impossible to separate taking from understanding. Crystal galleries contain the purest, richest salt deposits in the game - and the regrowing salt that proves containment heals if mining stops. Every salt crystal the player takes from an unmined gallery thins what they came to understand. The tension is explicit: the regrowing salt is both the most valuable resource AND the proof that taking is the problem. Salt hoarding here weighs heavily on ending availability.

**Connections**

- **From Area 2:** R68 Drain Access — requires **FIRE** (Skullflower) + pumps active → **R111**.
- **To Area 5:** **R144** Miner's Shaft Head → descend to **R146** (rope or repaired lift).

## Monsters (Area 4)

- **Shadows** — See in dark. **Devour light:** every few turns near a Shadow, the player's real light dims and can die. Coldfire and bioluminescence unaffected (already fake). True flame and normal lanterns vulnerable. Crystal lantern immune. Pass through doors.
- **Skullflower** (HP 12 base, scales) — No movement; propagates to unlit rooms; senses nearby (no vision); withers in true light; spreads through doors; **fire** kills; blocks passages physically.
- **Rats** (HP 3, ATK 1) — Breed; ubiquitous.
- **Bats** — See in dark; blocked by doors; minor threat.
- **Skeletons** (HP 16, ATK 7) — Abandoned mining crews; few; still executing last orders.
- **Demon** (HP 150, ATK 20) — Sealed; regenerates; multi-hit; destroys doors. **Conditional** — only if the player breaks the seal (R143).

### R143 — The Seal

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| **Era** | 2 |
| **Light** | Dark |
| **Enemies** | None (until seal broken) |

Circular chamber; **floor sigil**; ring of salt columns. Center: **stress** — hairline **cracks**, dust, warmth from below. Vast, patient — **not** a cartoon mind; **draw**, not rant.

**Inscriptions (readable):** The order's wards were built to **reinforce natural salt containment**, not replace it. **Mining thinned** the barrier; **wards compensated.** Wards **degrade**; salt **regrows** if left alone. The math was always binary: **stop mining**, or **maintain wards forever** — and forever is a lie institutions tell.

**Cracks (context):** Damage **correlates with necromantic throughput** — the lich's repairs stabilize one axis while **undeath's pressure** opens another. **Their solution stresses what it claims to save.**

**Examine / feelings:** Weight, warmth, **appetite without personhood**.

- **Connects to:** **R142**
- **Contains:** Seal inscriptions; deep lore; non-verbal **feelings** on examine
- **Special:** **MAJOR DECISION — Demon seal**
  - **Study** — Unlocks lich dialogue; affects endings. Reveals: the presence is **geological**, not sentient. It does not **push** — it **draws**. Salt accreted around the weak point like **calcium on an irritant**. If mining stops, salt **grows back**; natural containment can suffice. Ties to **R145** log: **thinning when people take**, **healing when they stop**; lich-era **ward reinforcement** vs. **crack expansion** under necromancy.
  - **Strengthen** — Cost consecration + salt crystal + **3 dungeon turns** ritual. Mends cracks; **fewer/weaker Shadows** in 4–5; weakens lich tricks tied to deep presence. Easier endgame.
  - **Break** — Destroy columns → **Demon** released (see Monsters). Rampage vs. lich forces; lich crippled; Demon remains a problem. **Ending 3 (Return — seal intact path) locked permanently.**
