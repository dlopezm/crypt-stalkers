# Area 5: The Founder's Reliquary

The dungeon's deepest point. A vast natural cavern containing an enormous salt-crystal formation — the deposit that started everything. The order built a shrine here. The lich made it their throne. All three eras visible in one space.

The final encounter is not a boss who wants company. It is an argument with someone whose data has been right for a century and a half — until the player finds the one column their ledger never had.

---

## Area Overview

**Emotional arc**: Awe (the crystal formation) → tension (elite defenses) → recognition (three eras in one space) → confrontation (the lich as systems, schedules, and justified cost) → the weight of choice → catharsis or consequence

**Learning sequence**: No new patterns. Everything the player has learned is tested simultaneously. The lich encounter mechanically tests: blunt (skeleton guard), fire (Skullflower barriers), light management (the lich suppresses regular light), ranged (back-row necromancers), consecration (lich's resurrection of fallen enemies). Mastery determines how the encounter plays out.

**NPCs**: Grandmaster Serevic (the Lich) — the game's most important conversation: precise, impatient, bitter, justified, diagnostically curious about the player. Mira's body (if she was refused help in Area 4).

**Capabilities tested**: ALL. This is the final exam.

**Bonus content**: The Mortal Quarters (R168–R171). Placing **Serevic's pre-transformation journal** (Area 2 R55) on the desk in R169 alters the encounter: the lich senses their own pre-lich calculations — including the hymn entry — and cannot hold the precise facade as cleanly.

**Connections**:
- FROM Area 3 R99 (Lich's Ward — force/knowledge/salt-iron) → R146
- FROM Area 4 R144 (Miner's Shaft — rope/repair) → R146
- No exit forward. The player leaves the way they came.

**Salt and endings.** The player's accumulated salt (player.salt) is the primary measure of greed at the ending. Salt hoarded = salt taken from the mine without spending on wards, braziers, or offerings. The lich's offer (Ending 1 partnership) is attractive partly because the player keeps their salt. Ending 4 (Release) requires low salt and demonstrated restraint. The game does not announce this - the salt counter just sits there, growing or not, and the endings respond.

---

## Grandmaster Serevic — Voice of the Encounter

Serevic is **not** lonely, fragile, or asking to be understood. They are a person who was **right** about a failing barrier, **right** about council incompetence, and who used being right to justify claiming everything.

- **Precise** — Systems, schedules, maintenance rotations. *"The northwest ward requires recalibration every 73 days. I have not missed one."*
- **Impatient** — *"The council said 'it's fine.' I showed them the numbers. They chose not to read them."*
- **Bitter** — *"I solved the problem your ancestors created. You're welcome."*
- **Justified** — Every decision defensible with evidence. *"Show me the error in the logic."*
- **Curious about the player — diagnostically** — *"What do you know? What can you do? Are you useful, or are you another taker?"*
- **Vulnerable ONLY to the hymn** — Nothing else fits the calculus. Performing it during the encounter cracks composure: anger first (*"Stop."*), then quiet, then something they cannot name.

The conversation is the emotional climax. Combat is optional failure or Ending 1 (fight). Arguing with better data than the player — until regrowing salt, the letter, the journal's hymn line, and the hymn itself prove there was always a number they chose not to run.

## Endings in Area 5 (Aligned With Master Design)

### Ending 1: Claim — Two Paths, Same Philosophy

*"It's mine. It was always mine."*

- **Fight:** Kill the lich. Take the mine. Epilogue: the cycle restarts; the heir's grandchildren face the same arithmetic.
- **Accept partnership:** Take Serevic's offer — surface and title for the player, deep maintenance for the lich. Comfortable, rational, **changes nothing**. The dead keep working.

Both answer **who pays?** the same way: **someone else.** The offer is **not** a separate ending. It is the polite version of greed.

**Requirements:** Combat strength (fight) or willingness to accept (partnership). Minimal knowledge suffices for combat-only routes; partnership may be offered whenever conversation allows the offer thread.

### Ending 2: Inherit

*"Someone must do this. I'll do it honestly."*

Take the vigil as a **living** guardian. Serevic transfers willingly and dies. The player stays underground forever. Better stewardship than undeath, but still a form of claim — still holding the mine.

**Requirements:** Deep knowledge — binding methods (library + restricted archive / colonnade), nature of the presence (demon seal **studied**), hymn performed during conversation so the lich believes the player understands the work.

**Conversation gate (moderate vs deep):** With **moderate** knowledge, Endings **1 and 2** become structurally available for negotiation (player can argue duty); full **inherit** resolution still needs the deeper checklist above.

### Ending 3: Return

*"None of this should exist."*

Collapse the mine permanently: crystal master array + consecration + seal knowledge. Destroy the resource. The town loses its economy. The cycle ends because there is nothing left to take.

**Requirements:** Crystal Master Array restored + consecration + demon seal **studied** + seal **NOT** broken.

### Ending 4: Release

*"The earth heals. Let it."*

Convince Serevic the salt **heals itself**. Present: regrowing salt (Area 4 crystal galleries), ancestor's letter (*"The salt keeps its own"*), the **hymn**, and **Serevic's pre-transformation journal** — especially the entry that broke the calculus (the hymn). Serevic resists: *"You're asking me to believe that 150 years of work was unnecessary."*

With **all** evidence + hymn + **emotional credibility** (low salt hoard, NPCs helped, restraint demonstrated) + demon seal **studied but NOT broken**:

Serevic is silent. Then: *"I knew. Somewhere, I always knew the salt was growing back. I chose not to measure it."* Coldfire dims. The undead stop. The mine goes quiet. The player walks out **carrying nothing**.

**Requirements (strict):** Regrowing salt witnessed/evidence carried; ancestor's letter; hymn knowledge (performance in encounter); Serevic's journal found and **presented**; seal studied, **not** broken; low salt hoard (salt spent on wards/braziers rather than hoarded).

**Deep knowledge gate:** Only with **deep** knowledge (seal studied, journal found, regrowing salt known) do Endings **1, 2, 3, and 4** all become available as branches in principle; flags and credibility determine which resolves.

## Subarea 1: The Outer Ward (6 rooms)

The lich's perimeter defense. Elite skeletons in ancient armor. The last line before the sacred space.

#### R146: Arrival Chamber
**Era 1+2+3 | Dark (coldfire flickers) | No enemies**

Two entrances converge here: the Miner's Shaft from Area 4 (a vertical descent landing on a stone platform) and the passage from the Lich's Ward in Area 3 (a corridor through broken bone columns). The room is a junction — raw mine walls (Era 1) reinforced with salt-block (Era 2) and marked with coldfire strips (Era 3). All three eras in one glance. The air is different here: heavy, still, charged. The crystal lantern's light refracts off salt in the walls.

- **Connects to**: Area 3 R99, Area 4 R144, R147, R162 (hidden — to Mortal Quarters)
- **Contains**: Mira's body (if she was refused help in Area 4 R127 — she's slumped against the wall near the Area 3 entrance, torch burned out, killed by the elite patrols. Her map and loot are on her body. The emotional cost is immediate.)
- **Special**: The hidden passage to R162 (Mortal Quarters) is behind a loose salt-block panel. It's visible only in bright light (crystal lantern at full) or if the player searches the walls deliberately. Mira's map (if she gave it to the player in Area 4) marks this passage.

#### R147: Guard Hall
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | Elite Skeletons ×3**

A ceremonial hall repurposed as a guard post. Three Elite Skeletons in ancient order plate armor stand in formation. They are the lich's personal guard — the oldest, most powerful skeletons in the dungeon. Salt-block pillars line the hall, carved with the order's flame motif (Era 2) overlaid with necromantic binding sigils (Era 3). The skeletons don't patrol; they hold position and attack anything that enters.

- **Connects to**: R146, R148, R149
- **Contains**: Ancient order weapons (high-quality longsword, ceremonial shield — good equipment), 30 salt
- **Special**: Three Elite Skeletons at once is a brutal fight. Blunt is essential (they reform otherwise). Strategy: lure one at a time by using light or noise in adjacent rooms to pull individuals from formation. Or: use consecration on the room after killing them to prevent reform entirely.

#### R148: Armory of the Dead
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | Elite Skeleton ×1**

Where the lich's elite guard stores its equipment. Weapon racks of ancient order weapons — well-maintained by undead hands. One Elite Skeleton sharpens a blade that doesn't need sharpening. The weapons here are the best conventional arms in the dungeon.

- **Connects to**: R147
- **Contains**: Order knight's blade (best slashing weapon in the game), reinforced shield, heavy crossbow + 8 bolts (better than the Area 2 crossbow), 20 salt

#### R149: Patrol Corridor
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | Elite Skeletons ×2 (patrolling)**

A long corridor connecting the Guard Hall to the Staging Ground. Two Elite Skeletons walk a route between R147 and R150. Salt-crystal inlays in the walls catch the coldfire, creating a faintly shimmering effect — the bones of the mine's beauty visible beneath the lich's occupation.

- **Connects to**: R147, R150
- **Special**: The patrol route is predictable. The player can time their movement between patrol passes. Or engage the skeletons in the corridor (narrow space, one-on-one advantage).

#### R150: Staging Ground
**Era 3 | Coldfire | Necromancer ×1, Zombies ×4**

Where the lich's forces muster before deployment to the rest of the dungeon. A Necromancer coordinates from a command position (back row). Four zombies stand in formation. Operational maps on the walls show deployment patterns across all five areas. A bone resonator connects to the Chapter House in Area 2.

- **Connects to**: R149, R151
- **Contains**: Deployment maps (full dungeon patrol overview), bone resonator (destroying this disrupts communication between Areas 2–5 for several dungeon turns), 15 salt
- **Special**: The Necromancer is in the back row — ranged is the optimal approach. Killing the Necromancer first makes zombies inert. Destroying the bone resonator is a strategic action that affects the rest of the dungeon (no reinforcements dispatched from here for a while).

#### R151: Inner Gate
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | No enemies**

A ceremonial gate marking the boundary between the outer defenses and the sacred space. The gate is open — the lich's ward at R99 was the real barrier. Here the architecture shifts: Era 2 religious construction becomes more ornate, more deliberate. The salt-block work is the order's finest. Inscriptions on the gate: "The Vigil endures. The Vigil provides. The Vigil consumes."

- **Connects to**: R150, R152
- **Contains**: Gate inscription (the third line — "The Vigil consumes" — was added later, in different handwriting. The lich's addition to the order's motto.)

## Subarea 2: The Prayer Colonnade (5 rooms)

The approach to the lich's sanctum. Salt-crystal columns inscribed with prayers that are actually binding formulas. Banshees bound to the columns. The ward system that protects the lich.

#### R152: Colonnade Entrance
**Era 2 | Coldfire | No enemies**

The colonnade begins. Salt-crystal columns rise from floor to ceiling, translucent, carved with text on every surface. In coldfire, the text is unreadable — the light level is too low. In true light (torch, lantern, crystal lantern), the inscriptions become legible: prayers to the order's patron, interspersed with mathematical formulas and geometric diagrams. The prayers ARE the formulas. The religion IS the binding.

- **Connects to**: R151, R153
- **Special**: The player must bring true light to read the columns. With coldfire only, this is an atmospheric corridor. With real light, it's a revelation: the order's faith was always partly a machine.

#### R153: First Inscription
**Era 2 | Coldfire | Banshee ×1**

A Banshee is bound to the third column — it orbits the crystal, wailing in a distorted hymn. The inscription on this column describes "the first duty: to hold what must not be released." A ward circle on the floor around the column glows faintly. Passing through the ward circle without understanding its geometry triggers defensive magic (3 damage, briefly stunned).

- **Connects to**: R152, R154
- **Special**: Knowledge gate (soft). The ward circle's geometry is described in the ritual texts from Area 2 R53. A player who studied those texts can identify the safe path through. A player who didn't takes damage but can still pass. The Banshee's wail echoes through the crystal columns — amplified, further-reaching than normal. Any fight here alerts R154–R156.

#### R154: Banshee Row
**Era 2 | Coldfire | Banshees ×2**

The densest section of the colonnade. Two Banshees orbit columns, their wails overlapping to create a dissonant harmony. The combined sound is physically painful — each turn spent here without killing or bypassing the Banshees deals 2 strength-drain damage. The columns here are covered in the most complex inscriptions: the binding formula's core mathematics.

- **Connects to**: R153, R155
- **Special**: Ranged is essential. The strength drain is cumulative. A player without ranged must endure multiple turns of drain while closing to melee. With ranged: kill the Banshees from R153 before entering (the crystal columns allow line of sight). The intersecting wails also trigger any Ghosts that might have been brought this deep (unlikely but possible via the Gutborn infection mechanic).

#### R155: Second Inscription
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | No enemies**

The final inscription column before the sanctum. This column has been modified — the original Era 2 prayers have been supplemented with Era 3 necromantic additions. The lich's handwriting is visible alongside the order's scribes'.

**Primary inscription (clinical, imposing):**  
*"The vigil does not end. The keeper does not rest. The salt remembers."*

**Below, in smaller, less precise handwriting — a maintenance note that accidentally admits more than intended:**  
*"Ward 7-NE recalibrated. Ahead of schedule. The silence is very complete tonight."*

The precision of the first lines and the stray loneliness in the second are both Serevic. The vulnerability leaks through logistics.

- **Connects to**: R154, R156
- **Contains**: Inscription (read: the lich is still running schedules — and still human enough to notice the quiet)

#### R156: Colonnade End
**Era 2 | Coldfire → Dark | No enemies**

The colonnade opens into a transitional space. The coldfire strips end. Ahead: darkness, and then — if the player brings light — the faint glimmer of something enormous. The crystal formation. The player stands at the threshold of the dungeon's final space.

- **Connects to**: R155, R157

## Subarea 3: The Order's Sanctum (6 rooms)

The order's holiest space, built around the natural cavern's crystal formation. Heavily modified by the lich. The transition from the approach to the throne.

#### R157: Sanctum Entrance
**Era 2+3 | Dark | Necromancer ×1 (Inner Circle), Elite Skeleton ×1**

A grand doorway carved from a single salt-block slab, now flanked by the lich's strongest servants. An Inner Circle Necromancer (HP 12, ATK 8 — stronger than standard) and an Elite Skeleton guard the entrance. The doorway's carving depicts the order's founding: figures building a shrine around a crystal formation. The crystal formation in the carving matches what lies ahead.

- **Connects to**: R156, R158, R161, R162 (hidden passage to Mortal Quarters)
- **Special**: Another entrance to the hidden passage to R162. Harder to spot than the one in R146 but present for players approaching from this direction.

#### R158: Altar Room
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | No enemies**

The order's primary altar. A massive salt-crystal slab, cracked, stained with centuries of ritual residue. Era 2 carvings on the altar's face show the Vigil Hymn's notation — the melody the player learned in Area 2. Era 3 modifications: necromantic symbols etched into the altar's surface, rerouting the altar's sacred function into the lich's power system. A sacred brazier stand beside the altar (extinguished).

- **Connects to**: R157, R159
- **Contains**: Altar inscriptions (the hymn again — confirming its importance), the desecrated altar (if consecrated by the player, it disrupts the lich's power slightly — the lich feels it and comments during the encounter, with impatience: *"You restored a ritual that maintains nothing. Efficient."*)
- **Special**: **Optional brazier.** If the player relights this brazier (using the hymn ritual), it floods the Altar Room and adjacent spaces with true light. This weakens the lich's control over light in the Crystal Throne subarea, making the final encounter easier.

#### R159: Relic Chamber
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | No enemies**

Where the order kept its holiest relics, now ransacked and repurposed. Display cases (empty — the lich took what was useful). But one case remains sealed — a consecration-locked case (needs CONSECRATION to open). Inside: a **blessed salt-iron amulet** — the most powerful anti-undead accessory in the game, providing resistance to necromantic magic and bonus damage against all undead.

- **Connects to**: R158, R160
- **Contains**: Consecration-locked case → **blessed salt-iron amulet** (powerful bonus for the lich fight), empty display cases (the lich took the rest), relic inventory list (shows what was here — context for the lich's resource hoarding)

#### R160: Brazier of the Deep
**Era 2 | Dark | No enemies**

The deepest sacred brazier in the dungeon. This one is special — it was the first brazier the order lit, and the last the lich extinguished. Its design is older and grander than the others. If relit, its effect reaches further: true light floods R157–R161 and weakens the lich's light-suppression capability in the Crystal Throne subarea.

- **Connects to**: R159
- **Special**: **Deep Brazier (major optional action).** Relighting this is harder than other braziers — the melody must be performed perfectly (the hymn learned from multiple sources throughout Area 2). Once lit, it dramatically changes the Area 5 endgame: the lich cannot suppress the player's light as effectively, the Crystal Throne is partially illuminated from this direction, and the lich's phase transitions are affected.

#### R161: Sanctum Gallery
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire | Elite Skeleton ×1**

A connecting corridor between the sanctum and the Crystal Throne. Salt-crystal walls with faded Era 2 murals showing the order's history: founding, growth, and — ominously — an unfinished panel where the painter stopped mid-stroke. The elite skeleton patrols slowly. The corridor opens into the dungeon's final space.

- **Connects to**: R157, R163

#### R162: Hidden Passage
**Era 1+2 | Dark | No enemies**

A narrow service passage connecting the Outer Ward (R146) and the Sanctum Entrance (R157) to the Mortal Quarters. Not on any map. Dusty. Quiet. The lich uses it because it is **efficient** — a private route that never needed repurposing.

- **Connects to**: R146 (hidden panel), R157 (hidden door), R168

## Subarea 4: The Crystal Throne (5 rooms)

The dungeon's heart. The natural crystal formation. The lich's seat. The final encounter.

#### R163: Throne Approach
**Era 1+2+3 | Dark (lich-suppressed) | No enemies**

The corridor opens and the cavern begins. The player's light reaches into darkness and catches the first glimpse of crystal — enormous, translucent, refracted. The central formation is ahead but the lich's power suppresses light here: regular torches and lanterns dim (not extinguished, but reduced to a few feet of glow). The crystal lantern resists the suppression — its light persists, reaching further, revealing more. The walls are all three eras layered: mine chisel marks under order carvings under necromantic inscriptions.

- **Connects to**: R161, R164
- **Special**: The lich's light suppression begins. Regular light sources are halved in range. The crystal lantern is reduced but functional. If the player lit the Deep Brazier (R160) or the Altar Brazier (R158), the suppression is weaker and regular light functions better.

#### R164: The Formation
**Era 1 (natural) + 2 + 3 | Dark (suppressed) | No enemies**

The central crystal formation. It fills the cavern's center — a towering cluster of translucent salt crystals, some taller than a person, growing from the cavern floor in a natural cathedral. The order carved prayers into the crystals' surfaces (Era 2). The lich added binding sigils (Era 3). The crystals themselves predate everything — this is what the miners found, what made the mine worth building, what the order declared sacred, and what the lich uses as power conduit and **phylactery host**.

If the player brings the crystal lantern to full brightness here (or has restored the Crystal Master Array in Area 4 to send light this deep), the formation responds: it catches the light and AMPLIFIES it, filling the cavern with refracted brilliance. The suppression breaks. Every inscription becomes legible. The lich's modifications are revealed for what they are: parasitic additions to a natural wonder.

- **Connects to**: R163, R165, R166 (revealed by light), R167
- **Special**: **THE LIGHT CLIMAX.** If the player achieves full illumination (crystal lantern + restored reflectors or relit deep braziers), this is the game's most dramatic visual moment. The cavern transforms from oppressive darkness to crystalline brilliance. Mechanically: the lich loses light-suppression advantage, the phylactery location (R166) is revealed, and the lich's first dialogue acknowledges it with controlled irritation: *"You increased ambient illumination. The formation responds to frequency, not sentiment."*

#### R165: The Seat
**Era 2+3 | Coldfire (controlled by lich) | Grandmaster Serevic (The Lich)**

The lich's position. A simple salt-block chair (not a throne — ostentation wastes maintenance cycles) facing the crystal formation. Serevic sits, hands on the chair's arms, watching the player approach. They have been waiting. They are not surprised — they have had 150 years to model intruders.

The room is small by comparison to the formation's cavern — almost intimate. Coldfire casts the lich's features in green. In true light: the lich's face is visible as it was — human once, now desiccated, the eyes still **sharp**.

- **Connects to**: R164, R166, R167
- **Special**: **THE LICH ENCOUNTER.**

**Phase 0 — Conversation.** Serevic speaks first (unless the player attacks immediately, skipping to Phase 1). Branching by **knowledge depth**:

- **Minimal** (rushed, combat-first, little lore): *"You came for the salt. Everyone comes for the salt. Take what you want from above. Leave my work alone."* → **Ending 1** only (fight or, if offered and accepted without deeper flags, partnership still counts as Ending 1 philosophy).
- **Moderate** (library, NPCs, surface history): *"You've read the histories. Then you know why I'm here. The barrier was failing. The council refused to act. I did the math. Show me the error in the logic."* → **Endings 1 and 2** available (claim paths + inherit negotiation).
- **Deep** (demon seal studied, regrowing salt known, pre-transformation journal found): *"You've seen the deep galleries. The unmined sections. You're going to tell me the salt is growing back."* → **Endings 1, 2, 3, and 4** available as the player meets each ending's flags.

**Key dialogue triggers (updated voice):**

| Trigger | Response sketch |
|--------|------------------|
| **Baron's signet ring** | *"An Ashvere. Your family started this. The debts, the deep mining, the collapse. Four hundred years later, here you are to take more."* |
| **Ancestor's bone tag** (player: family bones) | *"Yes. Crew 7, recovered from the collapse gallery. Processed and deployed to the northwestern ward. Still functional."* Cold, precise, devastating. |
| **Regrowing salt / Area 4 evidence** | The lich goes quiet. Then: *"The crystal galleries are not my operational priority."* Deflection. They know. |
| **Serevic's journal (presented)** | *"Where did you find that?"* If the player surfaces the **hymn entry**, Serevic cannot answer for a moment — the one line their spreadsheet never parsed. |
| **Hymn performed** | Composure breaks. Anger: *"Stop."* Then quiet. Then: *"The dead can't sing. I needed the dead more than the singing. That was the calculation."* |
| **High loot ratio** | *"You came here to take. Just like everyone. Just like me."* |
| **High consecration ratio** | *"You've been giving things up. That takes... I don't know what that takes."* |

**The offer (Ending 1 — partnership branch):** *"The mine was your family's. Take the title, reopen the upper galleries, manage the town. I continue the deep maintenance. All I ask: don't interfere below."* Sincere, rational, **mutually beneficial** — and it leaves the dead on the line. Accepting = **Ending 1 (partnership)**, not a separate "good" ending.

**Phase 1 — Combat** (if conversation fails or the player attacks). Serevic stands. The coldfire intensifies. Combat begins. HP 90, ATK 15.

The encounter tests every capability:
- Turn 1–3: Serevic summons Elite Skeletons (2 per turn). BLUNT is essential or the player is overwhelmed by reforming skeletons.
- Turn 4–6: Serevic raises any fallen enemies (zombies, skeletons). CONSECRATION prevents this. Without it, the fight is an attrition nightmare.
- Turn 7+: Serevic extinguishes all light in the room. CRYSTAL LANTERN is the only light that persists. Without it, the player fights blind while the lich sees perfectly.
- Throughout: Serevic casts from the back row. RANGED reaches the lich directly. Without it, the player must fight through summons first.
- Emergency: Serevic can cause Skullflower to erupt from the floor (the formation's crystal channels it). FIRE clears the Skullflower barriers.

If the player lit the Deep Brazier (R160) or achieved the Light Climax (R164), the lich's phases are weakened: fewer summons, slower resurrection, light suppression is partial. Knowledge and preparation are rewarded mechanically.

**Phase 2 — Journal on desk (R169) before fight.** If the player placed **Serevic's pre-transformation journal** on the desk in R169, the lich **senses** it before or as combat starts. The encounter **changes**: Serevic is shaken — someone holds their own pre-lich calculations, including the hymn line. They cannot maintain the precise, justified facade as cleanly. **Mechanically**, keep the same phase structure (summons, resurrection, light suppression, Skullflower) but alter timing or priority slightly (e.g. first summon wave delayed one turn, or one fewer skeleton on the first wave) and replace combat barks with interruptions (*"You read that. You brought that here."*). **Emotionally**, this is not pity — it is **exposure**. The player forced the lich to look at their own doubt in their own handwriting.

**Ending resolution** (conversation and/or aftermath):
- **Ending 1 (Claim):** Kill the lich (fight) **or** accept the partnership offer during conversation.
- **Ending 2 (Inherit):** During conversation, with deep knowledge and hymn performed, offer to take the living vigil; Serevic tests binding understanding; transfers and dies.
- **Ending 3 (Return):** Conversation path or post-lich resolution with array + consecration + seal studied + seal intact; collapse the deep workings.
- **Ending 4 (Release):** No fight; full evidence + hymn + credibility; Serevic accepts the salt heals; walk out with nothing.

#### R166: Phylactery Chamber
**Era 3 | Dark (hidden until lit) | No enemies**

A small cavity within the crystal formation itself, invisible in darkness. Only revealed when the formation is fully illuminated (crystal lantern at full brightness near the formation, or the Light Climax achieved).

Inside: the lich's phylactery — a salt-crystal orb containing a still-beating heart, preserved by necromantic salt. **It is not placed on a shelf. It has grown into the formation** — geology and lich fused. Serevic's immortality is **literal bedrock**; greed made them part of the mine.

Destroying it requires **light** (to find it) and **deliberate violence against the formation** — striking the crystal that holds the orb, breaking what the order called sacred and what the lich became. Without destroying it, the lich regenerates after "death" (bad variant: the player thinks they won, but the lich returns).

- **Connects to**: R164 (hidden within the formation)
- **Contains**: **The Phylactery** (destroying it before or during the fight ensures the lich stays dead. Without destroying it, Endings 1 and 3 epilogues may note lingering influence.)
- **Special**: Light reveals the truth. The central metaphor is mechanical: only by bringing light can you see what grew into the dark.

#### R167: Binding Circle
**Era 2+3 | Dark | No enemies**

The floor inscription around the lich's seat. A massive circle of carved binding formulas — the same mathematics from the Prayer Colonnade, rendered at full scale. This is the working the lich uses to interface with the formation and the deep presence.

**What deep study reveals (demon seal + library / colonnade knowledge):** The lich **draws power from the deep presence** to fuel immortality and **channels power back** to contain it. **Parasitic symbiosis.** If the presence were **naturally** contained by regrowing salt, the structural incentive shifts: the lich's power source weakens. Their existence **depends on the problem remaining unsolved** in practice.

This is **not** cartoonish conscious sabotage. Serevic **genuinely believes** the vigil is necessary. The bind is that the **architecture of their power** never rewards asking whether it still is. They cannot objectively evaluate the one hypothesis that would unmake them.

- **Connects to**: R165
- **Contains**: Binding circle inscriptions (why Ending 3 requires **collapsing everything** — you cannot cleanly decouple lich and depth with a single knife stroke — and why Ending 4's "salt heals" argument strikes the foundation, not just the rhetoric)

## Subarea 5: The Mortal Quarters (4 rooms)

A hidden side area. **Not a shrine.** Serevic is not sentimental about it. These rooms persist because **there was never a maintenance reason to repurpose them** — neglect dressed as efficiency. That makes the space **worse**: proof of a life filed under "no action required."

#### R168: Forgotten Corridor
**Era 2 | Dark | No enemies**

A dusty corridor. Footprints in the dust — **skeletal, recent**. The lich comes here. **Rarely.** They stand in the corridor or beyond; they do **nothing** operational. The efficiency expert visits the one wing that has **no function** on the schedule.

- **Connects to**: R162, R169

#### R169: The Preserved Room
**Era 2 | Dark | No enemies**

Serevic's bedroom from before the transformation. **Preserved by inaction**, not care. The bed is made because Serevic made it **150 years ago** and never had cause to unmake it. Desk, empty cup, bookmark in an **unfinished novel** — not curated; **abandoned mid-process** and never resumed. No shrine logic. Only the weight of **stopped time**.

- **Connects to**: R168, R170
- **Contains**: Personal effects (no monetary value; narrative weight).
- **Special**: **JOURNAL PLACEMENT.** If the player places **Serevic's pre-transformation journal** (Area 2 R55) **on the desk**, the lich **senses** it. The final encounter **changes** (see R165 Phase 2): their own calculations, including the hymn entry, **in the room they never repurposed** — the facade cracks before the player speaks.

#### R170: Personal Library
**Era 2 | Dark | No enemies**

Philosophy of **duty, sacrifice, competence** — not soft poetry of loss as the main note. Margin notes in Serevic's hand are **justifications in progress**: *"Is it greed to refuse to let something die? Is it duty to make yourself the only solution? What if no one else will do it?"* This is the lich's **actual** ethical engine: **competence as virtue**, the math that turned into monstrosity. A history of the barony (Ashvere line) may appear — studied as **operational context**, not apology.

- **Connects to**: R169, R171
- **Contains**: Notes that complicate easy judgment without asking the player to forgive.

#### R171: The Mirror Room
**Era 2 | Dark | No enemies**

A small side room. A full-length mirror **covered** — not from grief-as-trope but from **efficiency**: *"This serves no function."* Personal items on a shelf (comb, razor) are **artifacts of a discontinued process** — like the bedroom, left because there was no **work order** to clear them.

A **silver locket** remains: someone they **loved**. The room does not sentimentalize it; **the object is simply still there**. If the player removes the cloth from the mirror, they see a **living** reflection — warm, breathing — in a place built for the dead. No mechanical function. Evidence.

- **Connects to**: R170
- **Contains**: Silver locket, covered mirror
- **Special**: The mirror still asks the dungeon's last question: what does a living person **do** here — take, hold, break, or **walk away**?

## Implementation Notes

- **Light suppression** in R163–R167 is the lich's active ability. It scales with the lich's remaining power: destroying bone resonators, relighting braziers (R158, R160), and restoring crystal arrays all weaken it. A fully-prepared player faces minimal suppression. An unprepared player fights nearly blind.
- **The lich encounter** is **conversation first**; combat only if the player forces it or refuses all non-violent resolution. Serevic **wants** to **audit** the intruder — data first, blades second.
- **Ending availability** combines items, knowledge, rooms visited, loot/consecration ratio, NPC help, demon seal state, hymn completeness, and whether the pre-transformation journal was **found** and **presented** (and/or placed on R169's desk for the encounter modifier).
- **Mortal quarters** discoverable via hidden passages (R146, R157); Mira's map marks the R146 entrance.
- **Phylactery** in R166: grown into the formation; light + violence against the crystal to end the lich permanently.
- **Tone**: The player should feel they are **losing an argument on merits** until they bring **the hymn** and **the geology the lich chose not to measure**.
